Frequently Asked Questions

How much does attic insulation cost in New Haven?
Attic insulation in New Haven costs $1,400–$11,600. Blown-in fiberglass or cellulose for a 1,200 sqft attic (R-38 to R-49) typically runs $1,400–$4,000. Spray foam insulation costs 3–5x more but provides superior air sealing and is ideal for cathedral ceilings or unvented attics.
What R-value do I need for my attic in New Haven?
The DOE recommends R-38 to R-60 for attics in most of Connecticut. New Haven falls in climate zone Northeast — hotter climates need less, colder climates need more. If you currently have R-11 or less, upgrading to R-49 typically cuts heating/cooling costs by 15–20%.
How much can I save with attic insulation in New Haven?
Proper attic insulation in New Haven can reduce heating and cooling bills by 10–20%. For a home spending $200/month on energy, that's $240–$480/year in savings. Most attic insulation projects pay back in 3–6 years and come with significant utility rebates from Connecticut utilities.
Should I remove old insulation before adding new in New Haven?
If old insulation is damaged, contaminated (pest droppings, mold, vermiculite asbestos), or wet, remove it before adding new. For old fiberglass batts in good condition, blown-in insulation can usually be added on top. Removal adds $1.50–$2.50/sqft to the project cost in New Haven.
