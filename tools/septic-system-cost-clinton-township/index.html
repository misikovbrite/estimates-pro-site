Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a septic system cost in Clinton Township?
Septic system installation in Clinton Township costs $3,600–$31,600. A conventional gravity system with 1,000-gallon tank and drain field typically runs $3,600–$10,800. Mound systems (required for poor drainage) cost $10,800–$31,600. Aerobic treatment units for tight soils can reach $31,600.
How long does a septic system last in Clinton Township?
A well-maintained concrete septic system lasts 25–40 years. The drain field can last 25–30 years. In Clinton Township, pumping the tank every 3–5 years ($300–$600) and avoiding harsh chemicals dramatically extends system life.
Do I need a perc test before installing a septic system in Clinton Township?
Yes — almost all jurisdictions in Clinton Township require a percolation (perc) test to determine soil absorption rates before approving a septic system design. The test costs $300–$800 and is required before a permit is issued. Fast-draining sandy soils and very slow clay soils both affect which system type is approved.
What's the difference between a conventional and aerobic septic system in Clinton Township?
Conventional systems use gravity and natural soil bacteria — simpler and cheaper ($4,000–$15,000) but need sufficient land and adequate soil drainage. Aerobic systems (ATUs) add oxygen to accelerate treatment — required for poor soils or smaller lots in Clinton Township. ATUs cost $10,000–$20,000 but have a smaller footprint.
